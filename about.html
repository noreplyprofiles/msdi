About MSDI


Though not directly affiliated with the school, MSDI is run by current varsity MSJHS Speech and Debate members  in hopes of expanding the benefits of Speech and Debate to middle school students. The MSJHS Speech and Debate team is among the most competitively successful teams in the nation. Its members have championed multiple tournaments on the national circuit in the past few seasons and are nationally-ranked among tens of thousands others competing in all major Speech and Debate Events.

Each camp is 8 days long, from 6:00-8:00 pm. The tuition for each 8-day session is $225. Half the day will be dedicated to the Debate event chosen, while the other half will be reserved for the Speech event chosen. Students may choose to attend any of the two camps. All sessions will be held over Zoom.

Each student will get a chance to experience both a Speech and Debate event over the camp’s duration.  For speech, coaches will be helping students write and find a speech for the upcoming year. They will coach blocking, speaking, and acting. For debate, coaches will teach students case construction, research techniques, argumentation, and cross examination.

By enrolling in this camp, students will have an advantage over other competitors during the 2020-2021 Speech and Debate season. We highly recommend joining us over the summer for lots of fun, mock tournaments, and team bonding. We hope to see you there!

All proceeds of the MSDI program will be donated back to MSJ Speech & Debate.

Why Speech & Debate?


Speech and Debate encourage the development of important twenty-first century skills such as critical thinking, communication, collaboration, creativity and civic awareness. Additionally, research has even proven increased SAT and ACT scores. In fact, former US Secretary of Education Arne Duncan asserts that debate “boosts your college readiness—and your chance to succeed in life.” Speech and debate provide crucial skills when it comes to public speaking, research, and have even been shown to increase academic scores. In fact, recent alumni are attending elite universities such as Berkeley, Rice, Harvard, Stanford, and the University of Chicago. But most importantly, speech and debate is a part of our high school experience we will always treasure, and it’s never too early to get a head start!
